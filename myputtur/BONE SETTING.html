Herbal Healing

    

FEW things remain unchanged after 125 years. But Puttur seems to be caught in a time warp. It has taken perhaps a decade for the small railway station to get a roof over its head and for the lonely lodge in the town to get competition. Neither the radical transformation in Chennai, 105 km away, nor the slow modernisation in the temple town of Tirupati, 35 km away, seem to have affected Puttur, located close to the Andhra Pradesh-Tamil Nadu border. Adding to the town's aura of timelessness is the Bone-setting Hospital, in Easwarapuram two km from the railway station.

The Bone-setting Hospital is the reason why Puttur is known outside Andhra Pradesh. Here, four RMPs (registered medical practitioners) — as the doctors are called — toil from dawn to dusk fixing hundreds of fractures, from hairline to multiple, bone dislocations and arthritic bones to all those who come from all over the country.

Rudimentary infrastructure

To those used to the modern medical techniques, the infrastructure is a rude shock. No x-ray machines or scanners and no physiotherapist either. The hospital is a medium-sized room with four beds. The doctors decide the complexity of the fracture just by feeling it. The treatment is the same though the intensity and duration vary depending on the extent of fracture.

Once the expert’s fingers of the RMP identify the nature of fracture, the broken bones are adjusted, often a painful procedure, and bound with a green herbal paste. A cloth drenched in the herbal juice is tied around it. Bamboo sticks (brought from the nearby forest with government permission) are placed to ensure that the fractured bone does not move. The positioning of the sticks varies from fracture to fracture. A cloth is finally wrapped around the bamboo sticks.

After 15 days the bandage is removed, the fracture is reassessed and retied. This time, egg white is mixed in the herbal juice. The herb reduces the swelling, loosens the muscles and heals the bones, while egg albumin hardens the bone and tightens the muscles. Repeated checks are made and the bandage is replaced.
