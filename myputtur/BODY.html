PUTTUR

 Puttur is a Mandal Head Quarters in Chittoor District in the Indian state of Andhra Pradesh.

Geography

Puttur is located at 13°27′N 79°33′E13.45°N 79.55°E.

It has an average elevation of 144 meters (472 feet).

Puttur is situated on the way from Tirupati to Chennai.

Nearest Rail Head : Puttur Railway Station

 

To reach Puttur from Tirupati (35 km approx.):

By Train: Tirupati Railway Station (Sapthagiri Express, Tirupati Express, Garudadri Express).

By Bus: From Tirupati Bus Stand busses are available for every 30 minutes.

 

To reach Puttur from Chennai (110 km approx.):

By Train : Chennai Central Station (Sapthagiri Express, Tirupati Express, Garudadri Express)

By Bus: All busses going to Tirupati from Koyambedu in Chaennai (via Tiruttani / Uthukottai) stop here.

 

 Demographics

As of 2009, Puttur has a population of approx 80,000. Puttur has an average literacy rate of 85%, higher than the national average of 59.5%: male literacy is 84%, and female literacy is 73%. In Puttur, 11% of the population is under 6 years of age. Telugu its mother language of Puttur people.

The world famous Natural Bone Setting Hospital is located here. From ancient days, broken bones are set right by herbal leaves without operation, as it is one of the oldest procedures which gained popularity with the people all over the world. Though called puttur bone setters, this hospital is actually located in Rachapalem, a village near puttur. A family of kshatriyas discovered this unique procedure, and their descendants are still responsible for the management of this hospital.

The first Railway Track between Puttur and Renigunta is one of the earliest tracks in the present Andhra Pradesh. While laying the railway track, Tamil workers called this place "PUDHU OORU" which means "NEW PLACE". In due course of time, this name was changed to "PUTTURU" by the people in and around this place. Climatic conditions are very good and there is no pollution.

 

Education in Puttur

Puttur can be called the 'Education Hub' in Chittor (DT) after Tirupati. As most of the employees of this region prefer Puttur to stay with their families, this has led to adding up of a chain of educational institutions.
